YEARS EXPERIENCE
I have been officially working in the eSports industry since 2014, starting out as a commentator for Point Blank and Counter-Strike. Over time, I expanded my skill set to cover the full spectrum of eSports broadcasting. During my ten years of experience, I’ve organized and produced numerous online and LAN tournaments, serving in a variety of roles—observer, sound engineer, director, operator, and producer. You could say I’ve worn nearly every hat in this field.

In my work, I’ve introduced several innovative features to eSports broadcasting and am now eager to share my own software solutions. Previously, I implemented custom texture changes in CS:GO; if you heard about the team gloves at the MLG Major, that was my project. I also integrated 3D models into the game using SFM and Unity, collaborating with the WePlay team to bring these ideas to life.

Unfortunately or fortunately, after the full-scale war began in Ukraine, the company I worked for had to disband its broadcast team. Motivated by my ambition and experience, I decided to build my own team and create my own tournaments, continuing to push the boundaries of eSports broadcasting.
